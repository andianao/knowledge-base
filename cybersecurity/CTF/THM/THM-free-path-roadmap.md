# [Free TryHackMe Training: The Ultimate Guide for Beginners](https://tryhackme.com/resources/blog/free_path)

At TryHackMe, their guided content contains interactive exercises based on real-world scenarios.

While their [premium cyber security training](https://tryhackme.com/why-subscribe?ref=blog.tryhackme.com) offers the best learning experience with access to structured learning paths and unlimited training content, they believe that anyone and everyone should be able to learn. This is why they continue to release free learning content to ensure accessibility that caters to all. 

With over 500 free training labs and a series of free events throughout the year, they are making it easier to break into and upskill in cyber security!

Whether you’re new to cyber security or not sure where to get started with their training, they are here to help. To help you kickstart your learning, they have compiled a list of the free hacking training available to you, taking you from a beginner to an intermediate!

Before we dive in, they’d just like to explain that rooms on TryHackMe are broken into walkthroughs and challenges. Walkthroughs guide you and teach the skills required, while challenges test your skills, without any help. Let’s get started!

***Don’t forget!*** *You can now bookmark rooms to come back to them later on. Just click the bookmark icon in a room you'd like to save.*

### Level 1 - Getting Started

Before getting started with challenges and CTFs (Capture the Flags), they recommend easing in with the following training:

* [Careers in Cyber](https://tryhackme.com/r/room/careersincyber?ref=blog.tryhackme.com) - Learn about the different careers in cyber security
* [Intro to Offensive Security](https://tryhackme.com/room/introtooffensivesecurity?ref=blog.tryhackme.com) - Hack your first website (legally in a safe environment) and experience an ethical hacker's job
* [Introduction to Offensive Pentesting](https://tryhackme.com/module/introduction-to-offensive-pentesting?ref=blog.tryhackme.com) - Understand what a penetration test involves, including testing techniques and methodologies every pentester should know

* [OHsint](https://tryhackme.com/room/ohsint?ref=blog.tryhackme.com) - Use open-source intelligence to solve this challenge!

### Level 2 - Tooling

> Tooling refers to the specialized arsenal of software and scripts used to automate the discovery, exploitation, and analysis of vulnerabilities.

The most important thing in a pentester's toolbox is tooling. At this level, you’ll learn the absolute minimum of the necessary tools to become a better hacker!

* [Nmap Live Host Discovery](https://tryhackme.com/room/nmap01?ref=blog.tryhackme.com) - Learn how to use Nmap to discover live hosts using ARP scan, ICMP scan, and TCP/UDP ping scan
* [Hydra](https://tryhackme.com/room/hydra?ref=blog.tryhackme.com) - Learn about and use Hydra, a fast network logon cracker, to bruteforce and obtain a website's credentials
* [Linux PrivEsc](https://tryhackme.com/room/linuxprivesc?ref=blog.tryhackme.com) - Practice your Linux Privilege Escalation skills on an intentionally misconfigured Debian VM with multiple ways to get root!
* [Introduction to OWASP ZAP](https://tryhackme.com/room/learnowaspzap?ref=blog.tryhackme.com) - Learn how to use OWASP ZAP from the ground up (an alternative to BurpSuite)
* [Metasploit: Introduction](https://tryhackme.com/room/metasploitintro?ref=blog.tryhackme.com) - An introduction to the main components of the Metasploit Framework 

Again, here are some more introductory CTFs. These are a little more complex, but with your new knowledge of tools, you should smash them in no time! Don't worry if you can't, that's what hacking is all about – trying harder until you can no longer try and then learning from what you couldn't do.

* [Vulnversity](https://tryhackme.com/room/vulnversity?ref=blog.tryhackme.com) - Learn about active recon, web app attacks and privilege escalation

### Level 7 - Privilege Escalation

> Privilege Escallation is process of exploiting a bug, design flaw, or configuration error in an operating system to gain a higher level of access than originally intended. Typically, you start with a "low-level" user shell and must find a way to become **Root** (on Linux) or **System/Administrator** (on Windows) to read protected files like **/root/flag.txt**.

Privilege Escalation is essential to CTFs and hacking, so let's learn more about how to do it.

### Level 8 - CTF practice

> In the world of cybersecurity, a **CTF (Capture The Flag)** is a competitive exercise where participants solve complex security puzzles to find a specific piece of text, known as the **flag**.

These flags are usually hidden inside vulnerable websites, encrypted files, or compiled programs and typically follow a recognizable format like **thm{th1s_is_th3_fl4g}**. The competition serves as a legal and gamified way for hackers to sharpen their skills in areas like web exploitation, cryptography, and forensics, with the ultimate goal of submitting as many flags as possible to climb the **leaderboard**.
